1. 
Per decoding layer : 
    - Projections are O(t * d ^ 2), Attention terms are O(t ^ 2 * d), and hence the total complexity will depend on whether t or d is larger.
    Final formula =  O(t ^ 2 * d + t * d ^ 2) per step

2.
Total time complexity for computing T new tokens : 
  
At every time step, the context grows. Context will grow by T tokens, and since in q1 we figured that each decoding step is O(t ^ 2 * d + t * d ^ 2), then
we can deduce that ∑(t ^ 2 * d + t * d ^ 2) = O(T ^ 3 * d + T ^ 2 * d ^ 2)

3. 
Space Complexity : 
Each matrix (W_Q, W_K, W_V, W_O, W_ff1, W_ff2, W_embed, W_head) is d x d or smaller --> O(d ^ 2)
Attention Score matrix is t x t --> O(t ^ 2)

Therefore, O(t ^ 2 + d ^ 2)

4.

Idea : KV Caching
for the previously computed t - 1 tokens, we do not have to recompute the KV values again, and can just compute for the new t-th token.

Time : 
For the projection matrices --> computation now becomes computing KV for one token --> O(d^2)
For attention weights --> Q @ K^T --> shapes : (1,d) @ (d,t) --> O (td)
Total time complexity for one step : O(td + d^2)
then over T steps --> ∑ (td + d²) from t=1 to T = d·∑t + T·d² = O(T²d + Td²) which saves alot for long sequences

Space : 
now that we store the KV matrices at every layer, every time step, and KV has shape (t,d) --> eventual space complexity = O(Td)

Hence KV caching trades time (saving time) for space
